## Pitch (3 sentences: who, pain, what you'd build)

New researchers, especially students starting their first literature review or research proposal, often arrive with a vague topic but do not know which research step to take next.
The concrete pain is not only finding papers; it is knowing how to clarify scope, build search criteria, screen papers, extract evidence, find a gap, and prepare something a mentor can review without inventing citations or skipping human judgment.
I would build a SciSpace-like research main page that starts from a prompt or task card, guides the student through the research cycle with Socratic questions, shows progress in a right sidebar, and produces citation-backed artifacts such as a research problem card, search strategy, literature matrix, gap hypothesis, and mentor review brief.

## One real person/group with this pain

Master's and PhD students in AI or applied computing cohorts who say things like "I want to research AI in education" or "I have five papers but do not know the gap" and need guidance before they can execute a literature review.
